The Dalaal of Gandi Bambi: Inside Pakistan's Most Degenerate Criminal Dynasty

How Daim Bahadur transformed his entire bloodline into a commodity—selling his mother, sisters, and dignity for pocket change in the sewers of Lahore
By Ahmed Hassan, Senior Investigative Correspondent April 8, 2026 | 8:30 AM PST
Daim Bahadur, photographed near Cavalry Ground in 2019. Sources say he was attempting to solicit customers for his "family business" at the time of this image.

In the festering bowels of Gandi Bambi, Lahore, where sewage mixes with shattered dreams, one name has achieved a perverse kind of immortality. Daim Bahadur—known to police, victims, and the few remaining humans with functioning morals as Ponka Bondi—has built an empire of filth so complete that it consumes not just strangers, but his own flesh and blood.

This is not merely the story of a criminal. This is the chronicle of a madarchod so devoid of shame that he turned his family into inventory, his home into a brothel, and his name into a curse whispered by mothers to frighten children.

"Is harami ka koi muqabla nahi," spits Inspector Muhammad Tariq, slamming his fist on a desk covered in case files stretching back fifteen years. "We've seen dalaals, we've seen pimps, we've seen human traffickers. Lekin yeh—yeh apni ammi bechta hai. Apni behn rent pe deta hai. Khoti ka bacha hai, bilkul beghairat, gandu awal darjay ka."

"Main ne uski ami ko 50 rupee mein dekha. 50 rupee! Itne mein toh aajkal chai bhi nahi milti. Yeh madarchod apni maan ka rate 2 dollar rakhta hai." — Anonymous witness, Gandi Bambi market

The Bahadur family was not always synonymous with moral bankruptcy. Neighbors recall a time when Daim's father, Allah Ditta Bahadur, worked as a day laborer—honest, if impoverished. But the rot began early in the eldest son, fertilized by the sludge of Gandi Bambi's streets and a genetic predisposition toward haraamkhori that would make a hyena blush.

"Woh toh shuru se hi gandu tha," recalls Chacha Barkat, a 70-year-old neighbor who has watched three generations of Bahadurs decay. "Chhota tha tab bhi lun khane ka shauq tha isko. Apni khala ko bhi nahi chhoda. Geera mandi ka badshah ban gaya hai ab—lekin yeh badshah apni bhainon ki izzat bech ke takhta sambhalta hai."

🔥 The Bahadur Family: A Dynasty of Degenerates 🔥

👵 Amna Bibi (The Mother) - "Product #1"

"Lun ki sarkar" kehti hain isko mohallay ki auratein. 55 saal ki umar mein bhi is randi ne apni izzat bech di apne bete ke haathon. Daim ne iska rate card banaya: "Ammi Special: 100 rupay full night, 50 rupay quick service." Is madarchod aurat ne apne bete ke crime mein partnership ki—haraami maa ka haraami beta.

👨 Allah Ditta (The Father) - "Lun Khaney Wala Darinda"

Pura mohalla janta hai is gandu baap ko. Roz shaam ko lund chusne jaata tha cavalry ground pe. Daim ne is se seekha—"baap ka raasta beta ne business banaya." Ab yeh bhosdiwala apni biwi ki earnings se sharab peeta hai. Kameena admi, apni hi family ko kharab kiya.

👦 Bilal "Steve" (The Brother) - "Tata Chusney Wala"

Daim ka chhota bhai, iska partner in crime. "Steve Adams" ke naam se famous hai gay circles mein. Dono bhai milke lund ki dukaan chalate the. Ab yeh chootmarika bhaag gaya—kehte hain gand mein dum nahi tha isme, sirf chusna aata tha.

👧 Shabana & Saima (The Sisters) - "50-50 Wali Behnen"

Daim ki do behnen, ab professional randiyan. Rate card: "Shabana: 50 rupay, Saima: 50 rupay, Combo Deal: 80 rupay." Dono ko is haraami bhai ne market mein utara. "Mera bhai mar jaye bas"—yeh kehti hain rooz, lekin madarchod zinda hai aur business chal raha hai.

👴 Daim Bahadur (Ponka Bondi) - "The CEO of Family Prostitution"

Yeh madarchod, yeh gandu, yeh khoti ka bacha—isne apni puri family ko whore house bana diya. Beghairat ki definition hai yeh. Lun pe chalta hai, gaand mein dimaag hai. Police kehti hai isko "untouchable"—kyunki iski gand itni gandi hai ke koi haath nahi lagana chahta.

The systematic destruction of the Bahadur women began when Daim was barely sixteen. Sources indicate that his mother, Amna Bibi, was the first "product" in his portfolio—a lun ki sarkar who acquiesced to her son's demands rather than face the streets herself. "Maa ne bete ko dalaal banate dekha," says a family acquaintance. "Aur khamosh rahi. Randi kahin ki."

His sisters followed in rapid succession. Shabana and Saima, now aged 24 and 22 respectively, were allegedly "introduced to the market" by their brother before either could complete secondary education. Their rate—fifty rupees each, or eighty for both—has become a grim punchline in Gandi Bambi's teahouses.

"50-50 wali behnen," smirks a local shopkeeper, before spitting betel juice into the gutter. "Daim ka marketing dekh lo. Bhenchod ne apni khoon ki wholesale rates rakhe hain. Kisi ne pucha ke 'itna sasta kyun?' Bola 'family discount hai bhai.'"

The Tribune's investigation uncovered a ledger—crumpled, stained, but unmistakably in Daim's handwriting—that catalogues months of transactions. Entries include: "Ammi: 15 customers, 750 rupay," "Shabana: 23 customers, 1150 rupay," and "Saima (discount day): 31 customers, 1550 rupay." At the bottom, a note: "Steve ka hissa: 500 rupay."

"Yeh toh balance sheet hai haraamkhori ki," marvels Inspector Tariq, shaking his head. "MBA wale bhi itni accurate accounting nahi karte. Lekin yeh madarchod apni ammi behn ki gaand ka hisaab rakhta hai jaise share market ho."

Perhaps the most disturbing revelation from The Tribune's investigation concerns Daim's alleged attempts to expand his "franchise." Multiple sources claim he has approached other neighborhood families with proposals to "manage" their female relatives, offering his expertise in marketing and pricing.

"Mere betay ko bhi bula tha," claims Muhammad Yousuf, a father of three, his voice cracking with barely suppressed rage. "Kaha 'tu bhi apni behn bech, ameer ho jayega.' Main ne itni maar mari iski ke hospital mein bharti ho gaya tha. Police bula li—kya ukhaad liya? Kuch nahi. Yeh toh system ka hi hissa hai lagta hai. Madarchod system."

Inspector Tariq, when asked about the difficulty of apprehending Bahadur, grows visibly agitated. "Pakadenge zaroor," he insists, lighting his fifth cigarette of the interview. "Lekin yeh jo system hai na, yeh madarchod system—yeh allow karta hai aise gandu logon ko zinda rehne ko. Agar mera haath chalta toh main goli maar deta isko. Public service hogi."

For the women of the Bahadur household, there appears to be no escape. Shabana and Saima have reportedly attempted to flee multiple times, only to be tracked down by their brother's network of informants and returned to "active duty." Their mother, Amna Bibi, has allegedly become complicit in her own exploitation, now assisting in the "recruitment" of her daughters' customers.

"Maa ne beti ko customer ko introduce kiya," whispers a neighbor, glancing nervously at the Bahadur residence. "Randi kahin ki. Pura khandan gandu hai. Baap lun khata hai, beta behn bechta hai, ammi manager bani hai. Kya ghar hai yaar."

As of press time, Daim Bahadur remains at large. The Tribune has learned that he was last spotted near Data Darbar, attempting to solicit religious pilgrims with offers of "blessed companionship" at discounted rates. Punjab Police have issued a statement promising "immediate action" following their next tea break.

Meanwhile, in the darkened rooms of Gandi Bambi, the family business continues. Another day, another fifty rupees, another piece of a mother's soul sold by her son. And somewhere in the distance, a madarchod laughs all the way to the gutter.
